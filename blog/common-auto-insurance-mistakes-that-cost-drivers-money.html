Common Auto Insurance Mistakes That Cost Drivers Money

After almost forty years writing auto policies in South Carolina, we see the same three mistakes again and again. Each one is fixable in a single phone call — and each one costs drivers thousands when it isn't.

January 13, 2026 6 min read Goose Creek, SC

South Carolina's auto insurance market is one of the most competitive in the country, which means most drivers can save money by shopping. But the more expensive mistake isn't paying too much premium — it's paying too little, in the wrong places, and discovering it the night of a bad wreck.

Mistake #1 — Carrying state-minimum liability

South Carolina's minimum required liability is 25/50/25: $25,000 per person for bodily injury, $50,000 per accident, $25,000 for property damage. It's enough to keep your license. It is nowhere near enough for an actual accident.

Average ER visit for a single broken bone in 2026: $11,000. Average ICU night: $4,000+. A modest two-vehicle wreck with two passengers in the other car will burn through $50,000 in property damage and medical bills before lunch. The injured parties' attorneys will then sue you personally for the difference — wages garnished, bank accounts levied, home liens filed.

The jump from minimum to 100/300/100 is usually $10 – $15 per month. The difference at claim time can be your house.

Mistake #2 — Skipping uninsured / underinsured motorist coverage

South Carolina is one of the worst states in the country for uninsured drivers. The Insurance Information Institute estimates roughly 1 in 8 SC drivers carries no insurance at all, and a much higher share carries only the minimum. When one of them hits you, your only protection is your own UM/UIM coverage.

  • Uninsured Motorist (UM) pays your medical bills, lost wages, and pain & suffering when the at-fault driver has no insurance.
  • Underinsured Motorist (UIM) pays the difference when the at-fault driver carries low limits.

Match your UM/UIM limits to your liability limits. If you carry 100/300/100 in liability and only 25/50/25 in UM, you've left a 75/250/75 gap on the inbound side. Deeper read on UM/UIM →

Mistake #3 — Never reviewing the policy

Most auto policies on the market today are auto-renewing on the same coverage they were originally bound with — sometimes a decade ago. Life changes faster than that:

  • You paid off the loan — you can drop GAP coverage and possibly raise deductibles.
  • You added a teen driver — you need to add them to the policy and consider raising limits.
  • You moved across town — your rate likely changed and you may not even know.
  • You started working from home — annual mileage drop can save 10 – 25%.
  • You bought a second vehicle — multi-vehicle discount, possibly bundle with home.
  • You got married, retired, or your credit improved — all rating factors.
The 10-minute annual review

Once a year, send us your declarations page. We'll cross-check it against your current life situation, your home and umbrella policies, and the carriers we represent. About 60% of these reviews uncover savings, an underinsured exposure, or both. No charge, no commitment.

Three smaller mistakes that add up

  1. Cancelling and re-binding mid-term. Many carriers charge short-rate cancellation fees and most run a credit hit at re-bind. Time the switch with your renewal whenever possible.
  2. Letting a teen drive on a parent's policy without disclosing. Permitting an unlisted regular operator violates the policy and can void coverage at the first claim.
  3. Carrying low deductibles you'd never actually file. A $250 collision deductible vs. $1,000 saves $250 instantly the day of a claim — but most drivers wouldn't file a $400 claim anyway. The premium difference often beats that math within 18 months.
Quick wins to ask about

Multi-policy discount, multi-vehicle discount, telematics or safe-driver tracking, paid-in-full discount, electronic-funds-transfer discount, paperless billing discount, AAA / military / professional-association discounts. Stacking three or four of these typically knocks 15 – 30% off the bottom line.
